Battery power is used whenever Pokemon Go is active on a device, and can often eat up more battery life than desired or expected.

How to Reduce Battery Consumption for Pokemon Go

In addition to eating up a bunch of your data when not on wifi, Pokemon Go is a gluttonous monster when it comes to battery life.

There are a few ways to avoid using an excess amount of battery power when playing Pokemon Go.

Close Background Apps

When running Pokemon Go, avoid doubling up on data usage by closing any network-enabled apps that may be running in the background.

Dim Screen Brightness

The brighter your screen, the more battery you'll use when playing.

Disable Sounds

Not only is turning the volume on your mobile device a good step towards saving battery life, also consider disabling all audio effects in the app itself.

  1. Open the m in-game menu (tap the poke ball on the map screen).
  2. Selecting the Settings tab in the top-right corner.
  3. Disable Music and Sound Effect
  4. Enable "Battery Saver" mode

How Battery Saver Works

Just enabling Battery Saver won't do much of anything. However, if you then hold your phone upside down (in your hand or pocket) the screen will darken and display a faint Pokemon Go logo. In this mode you won't be able to see anything, but the game will still track you on GPS, pedometer count for hatching eggs, and vibrate if any pokemon nearby pop up - making it a better alternative to minmizing the game (which doesn't track anything)

Turn off WiFi and Bluetooth

While out roaming the streets or woods - i.e. not able to connect to reliable WiFi - disable your phones WiFi / Bluetooth functions (unless they're currently in use).

Constantly searching for and attempting to connect to Bluetooth or WiFi signals will greatly contribute to your battery usage, so eliminate it whenever possible.

Download Local Maps

Some users have reported that downloading their local area maps from the Google Maps app has significantly reduced their battery usage (since Go pulls from Google Maps for it's location data).

To download your area maps, complete the following steps:

  1. Open Google Maps app on the device you use for Pokemon Go.
  2. Open the Settings menu.
  3. Select 'Offline Areas'.
  4. Press the '+' button.
  5. Size the on-screen map around the area you want to download.
  6. Select 'Download'.

It's worth noting that Google engineers have said this , but many users maintain reduced battery consumption after saving these maps.
