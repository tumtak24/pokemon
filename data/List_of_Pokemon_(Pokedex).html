Pokemon Go allows you to use the GPS on your smartphone to find Pokemon in the real world. As you go about your normal life, your smartphone will vibrate to let you know a Pokemon is nearby. Use your smartphone's touch screen and throw a Poke Ball to capture the wild Pokemon.

 

Some wild Pokemon only live in certain locations. You may need to go to a lake or ocean to find water-type Pokemon, or to a local park to find grass-type Pokemon.

 

Eggs

As you explore with Pokemon Go, you may come across a Pokemon Egg at a PokeStop. Carry the Egg with you and it will hatch after enough steps have been taken. The Pokemon inside may be a species you've never encountered before. For more information see the Eggs and Hatching page.

Pokemon Types

All 18 types will be available in Pokemon Go. This includes Bug, Dark, Dragon, Electric, Fairy, Fighting, Fire, Flying, Ghost, Grass, Ground, Ice, Normal, Poison, Psychic, Rock, Steel, and Water.

Currently no Pokemon available has the Dark type.

Moves

Unlike other Pokemon games, each Pokemon will have two types of move, a basic move and a special move. These moves are only used in gym battles, where combat takes place in real time, and is no longer turn based. In battle, tapping on the Pokemon will cause it to perform it's basic move as soon as it is able. Doing this will charge special bars below the Pokemon's health that can be used to perform the Pokemon's special move. Different special moves have different quantity bars and different lengths to fill.

Note that the same Pokemon can often have different moves, so be sure to check!

Levels

In Pokemon Go, Pokemon do not have levels, but a new identifier called Combat Points (CP). Pokemon caught will often have random Combat Points, and a trainer's level can often influence more experienced Pokemon. In order to increase a Pokemon's CP, a player can use Stardust and Pokemon Candy to power up a Pokemon, increasing its CP. They can also evolve the Pokemon for an increase of total CP.

Evolution

Pokemon evolve much the same way that they gain Combat Points - by using Pokemon Candy. Each Pokemon caught comes with a few candy for that type of Pokemon, and more can be gained from catching more, hatching them from eggs, or transferring duplicate pokemon (which releases them from your storage).

Each Pokemon needs a different amount of candy to evolve, so be sure to keep catching the same kind of pokemon and transfer them to build up stockpiles of candy.
