This page contains information on data usage for .

Cellular data is used whenever Pokemon Go is active on a device not connected to a WiFi network.

Data Usage

In our experience, it seems like Pokemon Go uses between 4MB and 8MB of cellular data per hour of  active playtime (meaning when the app is open on-screen, not in the background). Depending on the specific device being used for play (Android, iPhone, etc) and the network connection (3G, 4G, LTE), data usage may vary.

You can see how much data the app has used so far (and measure how much it takes up for you) by taking the following action:

On Android

  1. Go to 'Settings'.
  2. Open 'Data Usage'.
  3. Set your Date Range.
  4. Scroll down to locate the Pokemon Go information.

On iPhone

  1. Open Settings menu.
  2. Select "Cellular".
  3. Scroll down to Pokemon Go information.

How to Reduce Data Usage for Pokemon Go

There are a few ways to avoid using an excess amount of cellular data when playing Pokemon Go.

Play on Wifi

This may seem obvious, but you'd be surprised how often people forget to connect to an available WiFi network.

Make sure to connect to a WiFi network whenever possible while hunting Pokemon. Of course, use proper judgement when connecting to public networks and hotspots.

Download Local Maps

Some users have reported that downloading their local area maps from the Google Maps app has significantly reduced their data consumption (since Go pulls from Google Maps for it's location data).

To download your area maps, complete the following steps:

  1. Open Google Maps app on the device you use for Pokemon Go.
  2. Open the Settings menu.
  3. Select 'Offline Areas'.
  4. Press the '+' button.
  5. Size the on-screen map around the area you want to download.
  6. Select 'Download'.

It's worth noting that Google engineers have said this , but many users maintain reduced data consumption after saving these maps.

Close Background Apps

When running Pokemon Go, avoid doubling up on data usage by closing any network-enabled apps that may be running in the background.
